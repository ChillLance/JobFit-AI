# ADR-2026-001: Validate a Japan working-holiday resort-job wedge

- Status: working decision; validate before treating as final
- Date: 2026-07-14
- Scope: product positioning, profile/search-intent design, job normalization,
  fit analysis, application assistance, and automation policy
- Decision:
  Focus the first specialized JobFit-AI validation loop on Traditional-Chinese
  users seeking live-in resort or hospitality work during a Japan working
  holiday. Differentiate through cross-source normalization, hard eligibility
  gates, estimated savings and living-fit comparison, missing-information
  questions, and verified application assistance. Keep every send or submission
  human-confirmed.
- Constraints:
  - Resume tailoring is a supporting feature, not the product position.
  - Do not build a generic job board or mass auto-apply system.
  - Treat tech/remote and long-term Japan-career searches as separate modes.
  - Do not infer visa or legal permission from free text; surface uncertainty
    and official sources for user confirmation.
  - Preserve verified career facts and trace generated claims back to them.
  - Validate with real listings and target users before adding auth, cloud sync,
    universal crawling, or unattended automation.
- Consequences:
  - Positive: the product serves a narrower user with Japan-specific decisions
    that generic resume tools and job boards do not cover end to end.
  - Positive: the existing extension, SQLite repositories, profiles, fit
    analysis, salary support, and application tracking remain reusable.
  - Negative: resort-job extraction requires a richer model for housing, meals,
    transport, work patterns, contract dates, and missing facts.
  - Negative: the wedge depends on user research and source access; market
    signals do not yet demonstrate willingness to pay.
- Supersedes / Superseded by: none. Supersede this ADR if validation rejects the
  wedge or if the user explicitly chooses a different primary market.
